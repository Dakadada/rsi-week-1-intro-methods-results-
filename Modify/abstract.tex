Short-peptide self assembly predictive modeling requires structured datasets, yet experimental parameters remain scattered across literature. Manual extraction is unscalable, and direct Large Language Model (LLM) extraction doesn't resolve a lack of parameters or overlaps between experiments. We develop GraphPeptideMiner, a multi-agent extraction workflow that processes raw PDFs to construct evidence-grounded knowledge graphs. Specialized agents map sequences and conditions to observed phases. We hypothesize this graph-grounded architecture improves extraction accuracy, traceability, and downstream phase prediction compared to direct LLM and manual baselines.
